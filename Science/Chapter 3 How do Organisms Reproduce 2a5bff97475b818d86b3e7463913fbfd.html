Reproduction

  • Reproduction is the process by which living organism produce new individuals of the same species.
  • It is not an essential life process.

Why it is necessary?

  • It ensures the continuity of a particular species on earth.
  • To stable population.
  • For evolution.
  • Variation would never be found in the same species.

Variation

  • Variation are the differences among individuals of the same species.
  • Occurs due to changes in Genetic Material in Nucleus.
  • Cells contain Nucleus, and in Nucleus there are chromosomes which contains DNA(Deoxyribo Nucleic Acid).
  • DNA carries the information for making proteins which determines the characteristics.
  • If there are changes in DNA the proteins changes which results in new characteristics.
  • DNA forms its copy DNA.
  • When there is a mistake in the DNA copy its results in new DNA structure.

Vegetative Propagation

  • Parts of plants like roots, stem and leave are cut and planted which develop into new plants.
  • Vegetative Propagation by Stem: Potato, Onion, and Lemon.
  • Vegetative Propagation by Root: Sweet Potato, Guava.
  • Vegetative Propagation by Leaf: Bryophyllum.

Advantages of Vegetative Propagation

  • Plants raised can bear flower and fruits earlier than those produced from seeds.
  • Plants can be produced in large number in less time.
  • It is more rapid, easier and cheaper method of multiplication of plants.
  • Due to this propagation of plants like banana, orange, rose and jasmine is possible which have lost their capacity to produce seeds.
  • All plants are genetically similar enough to the parent plant.
  • Desirable character of fruit can be maintained.

Sexual Reproduction

  • Fusion is called Fertilization more specifically.

Why the Sexual Mode of Reproduction?

  • Combine variations from two parents of same species.
  • Increase chances of survival by creating more variety in population.

Sexual Reproduction in plants

Male Reproductive part: Stamen (Anther and Filament).

Female Reproductive Part: Pistil (Stigma, Style, Ovary, Ovule).

Petals attract insect for pollination due to its scent and colour.

Types of Flower:

Bisexual FlowerUnisexual Flower
Contains both stamen and pistil.Contains either stamen or pistil.
Examples: Hibiscus, Mustard.Examples: Papaya, Watermelon.

How Reproduction is done

  1. Anther contains pollen grain.
  1. Pollen lands on stigma.
  1. A tube grow out of the pollen grain.
  1. Male gamete travel to ovule.
  1. Male gamete and female gamete do fertilization and forms zygote.
  1. This zygote will be become embryo.

Germination

  • Process by which a seed starts to grow into a new plant.
  • It is the development of seed into seedlings under appropriate conditions.

Pollination

  • Transfer of pollen from anther to stigma of flower.
  • Agent of pollination are wind, water and animals.
  • Two Types: Self Pollination and Cross Pollination.

Reproduction in Humans

Sexual Maturation of Body: Puberty

  • Adolescence: The phase of life between childhood and adulthood.
  • Puberty: The time in life, when a boy or a girl becomes sexually mature. it is a process that usually happens between ages of 10 and 14 for girls and age 12 and 16 for boys. It causes physical changes, and effects boys and girls differently. his period during adolescence, generate rate of growth decreases.

Male Reproductive System

  • Male gamete is called Sperm.

Female Reproductive System:

  • Female gamete is called Ovum.

Sexual Intercourse:

  • Sperm enters through the vaginal passage.
  • Travel upwards.
  • Reach fallopian tubes.
  • Encounter egg.
  • Fertilizes it.

Fertilization:

  • Fertilized egg (zygote) divides to form ball of cells.
  • Implanted in the lining of the uterus.
  • Grows and Develop organs to become foetus.

Menstruation

  • The process of shedding the uterine lining leading to vaginal bleeding on a regular monthly basis.
  • The ovary releases one egg every month.
  • The uterus also prepares itself every month to receive a fertilized egg.
  • Thus its lining become thick and spongy for nourishing the embryo if fertilization had taken place.
  • However, the lining is not needed any longer if fertilization does not occur.
  • Menarche is the stage of beginning of menstruation.
  • Menopause is the stage of stoppage of menstruation.
