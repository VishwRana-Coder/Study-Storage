Steps in Metallurgy

Step 1: Crushing and Grinding of Ore

  • Ore is grinned and crushed into small pieces or powder.
  • Purpose is to make extraction of metal easier and efficient.

Step 2: Enrichment of Ore

  • Gangue is removed.
  • Increase the percentage of metal of ore.

Step 3: Extraction of Metal

  • High reactivity metals (K, Na, Ca, Mg, Al) are extracted by electrolysis.
  • Medium reactivity metals (Zn, Fe, Pb) are extracted by reduction with carbon/CO, or displacement.
  • Low reactivity metals (Cu, Hg, Ag, Au) are found in native state or extracted by mild reducing agents.

Extracting Metals of Low Reactivity

  • Metals of low reactivity are very unreactive.
  • Their oxides can be converted into metals by heating alone.

Extracting Metals of Medium Reactivity

  • Metals like iron (Fe), zinc (Zn), lead (Pb), copper (Cu) are moderately reactive.
  • They can be found in sulphide or carbonate ores.
  • They are converted into oxides first then reduced by a more reactive metal.

Thermit Reaction

  • The heat evolved is so high that the iron is obtained in the molten state.
  • Aluminium acts as a reducing agent because of its high reactivity.
  • This reaction is used for joining railway tracks.
  • It is also used for repairing cracks in machine.

Extracting Metals of High Reactivity

  • Metals high up in the reactivity series (K, Na, Ca, Mg, Al) are very reactive.
  • They cannot be reduced from their oxides using carbon because they have more affinity for oxygen than carbon.
  • These metals are obtained by Electrolytic Reduction of their molten salts.

Refining of Metals

  • Metals obtained by reduction processes are not pure and contain impurities.
  • Electrolytic Refining is the most widely used method.
